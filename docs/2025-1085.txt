5.4   SmallWood for Small Fields
It can be observed from the soundness analysis and the scheme parameters that, in its basic form, SmallWood-
ARK is particularly well-suited for large fields, typically those of size significantly greater than 2λ (e.g. fields of
size 22λ ). In such settings, the soundness error ε3 can be made negligible by opening just a single polynomial
evaluation from the PCS, i.e., by setting the parameter ℓ′ = 1. This is desirable, as ℓ′ has a substantial
impact on the overall proof size. However, for smaller fields, achieving negligible ε3 may require a larger
value of ℓ′ , resulting in a heavier proof. To address this, we introduce below a variant of SmallWood-ARK
specifically tailored for a “small” base field F.
    In this variant, the PACS statement remains unchanged from the original formulation, with the underlying
witness matrix still defined over the base field F (see Section 5.1). However, the witness vector polynomial P
and the masking vector polynomial M are now defined over an extension field K of F. Let θ denote the
extension degree of K over F. The size of K is chosen such that only a single polynomial evaluation needs
to be opened, i.e., ℓ′ = 1. Such a choice also implies that the dimension ρ of the vector Q can be set to 1
while ensuring a negligible soundness error ε2 = 1/|K|ρ . In consequence, only a single masking polynomial is
needed. We therefore simplify the notation and write Q and M instead of Q and M . In the first step of this
                                                                                              ′
adapted PACS PIOP, the prover constructs random polynomials P1 , . . . , Pn ∈ K[X](≤ℓ +s−1) such that their
evaluations Pi (ωk ) = wi,k belong to the small field F ⊆ K for all i ∈ [1, n] and k ∈ [1, s]. The remainder of
the PACS PIOP follows the original protocol (see Figure 6), with the base field F replaced by K, and with
the evaluation set S redefined as K \ Ω.
    The SmallWood-PCS scheme is then adapted to leverage the fact that, although the committed polyno-
mials P1 , . . . , Pn are defined over K[X], they admit s evaluations (on the witness support Ω) in the small
field F ⊆ K. To exploit this, we commit these K[X] polynomials using SmallWood-LVCS over the small field F.
Specifically, we adapt the construction of the matrices Aj to commit K[X] polynomials while defining their
entries over F. Let ϕ be the F-linear field embedding isomorphism from Fθ to K. Recall that the witness sup-
port is given by Ω = {ω1 , . . . , ωs } ⊆ K, and choose an additional distinct evaluation point ωs+1 ∈ K \ Ω for
teh randomness support. To commit a witness polynomial Pj , we adapt the matrix Aj from SmallWood-PCS
(see Section 4.2) as a single column of dimension s + θ, defined as Aj = (a1 , . . . , as+θ )T with

                (a1 , . . . , as ) = (Pj (ω1 ), . . . , Pj (ωs ))   and   (as+1 , . . . , as+θ ) = ϕ−1 (Pj (ωs+1 )) .

Pj is encoded as a single-column matrix, no extra randomness is required to mask the result of the linear

number of columns ncols in the global LVCS matrix is a free parameter from which the remaining parameters
are derived. Specifically, we obtain ⌈n/ncols ⌉ layers of s+θ rows to commit P1 , . . . , Pn . The first layer consists
